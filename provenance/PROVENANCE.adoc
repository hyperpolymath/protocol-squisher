This document describes the contents of `provenance/`, how the work came to be,
what it established, and where it failed. It is written so that the archive can
be read as evidence without re-running it. Every factual claim cites a file in
this archive by relative path, or a named sibling repository.


== What this directory is

`provenance/` is a complete, unedited archive of Protocol Squisher as it stood at
versions v1.x through v1.2 (developed January–June 2026): roughly thirty-five
Rust crates under `crates/`, thirteen schema analysers, proof artefacts under
`proofs/` across six provers (Agda, Coq, Idris2, Isabelle, Lean, Z3), an
arXiv-style paper
(`arcvix-transport-class-lattice.tex`), two empirical-validation harnesses, and
the supporting documentation. It is preserved as evidence, not maintained as
software. Nothing under `provenance/` is built, tested, or kept current; the one
change made to the tree at the point of archiving was the addition of a single
Lean theorem (`hub_ceiling`, see <<closing>>), committed before the move. The
root of the repository above this directory is a fresh start.

== State of the field at the start (January 2026)

The project began at the intersection of two literatures. The first — graded and
bidirectional structure — is the one the original lineage notes recognised
(`INTENT.md`, "WHAT the intellectual lineage is"). The second — the algebra of
bottlenecks — is the one that turned out to be decisive, and which `INTENT.md`
does not cite. This section harvests the first from `INTENT.md` and corrects the
record by adding the second.

=== Graded and bidirectional structure (recorded in INTENT.md)

* *Bidirectional transformations and lenses.* Symmetric lenses
  (Hofmann, Pierce, Wagner, 2011) and delta lenses (Diskin et al., 2011) supply
  the notion of information-preserving maps between two representations. The
  transport classes were read against these: an isomorphism, an embedding, a
  projection (`INTENT.md`, "Bidirectional Transformations").
* *Functorial data migration* (Spivak, 2012) — schemas as categories, instances
  as functors, migrations as natural transformations (`INTENT.md`, "Categorical
  Database Theory").
* *Information effects* (James, Sabry, 2012) — information-theoretic reasoning
  about type isomorphisms, behind the `InformationContent` type
  (`INTENT.md`, "Information Theory meets Type Theory").
* *Profunctor optics* (Pickering, Gibbons, Wu, 2017; Kmett's `lens`) — the
  product/sum/recursive/dependent structure of the Shape IR is read as a
  hierarchy of optics (`INTENT.md`, "Optics").
* *Practical systems already in the field.* Cambria (Ink & Switch) for
  lens-based schema evolution; `buf.build` for Protobuf breaking-change
  detection; Apache Arrow as a typed columnar schema; and CUE's lattice-based
  unification of types and values (`INTENT.md`, "Practical Systems"). Cambria is
  the nearest prior system; the project's stated novelty was generalising beyond
  JSON documents to arbitrary data shapes.

=== The algebra of bottlenecks (absent from INTENT.md, decisive in the end)

The transport classes compose by taking the worse of two steps. That is the
*min–max* or *bottleneck* semiring, an idempotent semiring (a dioid), and the
problem of finding the highest-fidelity adapter path is the classical *minimax
path* problem. This mathematics long predates the project:

* Pollack (1960) and Hu (1961) — the bottleneck / minimax path problem.
* Carré (1971) — path algebras over semirings for routing.
* Cuninghame-Green (1979) — minimax (and max-plus) algebra as a coherent theory.
* Mohri (2002) — shortest-distance algorithms over general semirings, which
  subsumes the bottleneck case.

This thread is the one the project eventually reached
(`proofs/tropical/TropicalAdapterPath.lean`, dated 2026-04-11), and it is the
part that yielded a definite result. It is recorded here because `INTENT.md`'s
lineage section, written 2026-03-10, frames the work entirely through lenses,
categories, and optics and never names the bottleneck-semiring literature in
which the transport-class result actually lives.


== The need

Software speaks many serialization formats at once — Protobuf, Avro, Cap'n Proto,
Thrift, JSON Schema, and the rest — and moving data across them is error-prone
manual work. The tooling that exists answers compatibility as a yes/no question:
two schemas are binary-compatible or they are not. That distinction is too coarse
to act on. Most real conversions are neither perfectly safe nor outright
impossible; they lose something specific and bounded, and the practitioner needs
to know what.


== The theory challenge

Can compatibility be *graded* rather than declared — can the loss in a conversion
be measured on a scale, and, crucially, can those measures be *composed* so that
the loss along a chain of conversions is computable from the losses of its steps?
The transport classes (Concorde, Business, Economy, Wheelbarrow) are one such
grading, and their composition rule is the subject of the theory.


== The practical challenge

Can adapters between two shapes be *synthesised automatically* — derived rather
than hand-written — with the loss they incur documented, and at a fidelity high
enough to be worth using? The `crates/protocol-squisher-minikanren/` synthesiser
and the thirteen analysers are the attempt at this.

== What went wrong

This section is a methodology case study, for a reader who wants to avoid the
same failures when working with AI assistance at speed. It is specific and
cited. It is neither an apology nor a defence.

*The validation harness was built twice and never fired.* Two separate empirical
pipelines were constructed to test the project's claims against real schemas: the
in-repo crawler under `explorer/` (an Elixir/OTP GitHub schema crawler;
`explorer/README.md`) and a standalone sibling repository, `squisher-corpus`.
Neither produced data. In `squisher-corpus`, the machine-readable state file
`.machine_readable/6a2/STATE.a2ml` records `completion-percentage = 0` and
`phase = "In development"`, while its prose dashboard (`TOPOLOGY.md`) reports
"~70%" overall with several components marked "100% ... verified". In this
archive, `explorer/database/` contains only a `README.md` — no collected
schemas, no exports. The instrument that would have connected the theory to
reality was assembled, twice, and never switched on.

*Hypotheses were tested against self-authored examples.* The February 2026
diversity findings were computed over roughly one hundred example schemas written
for the purpose, not sampled from the world (`ANALYSIS-COMPLETE.txt`: "Pattern
frequency analysis (8 patterns, 100+ schemas)"; `DIVERSITY-ANALYSIS.md`,
Appendix A, "Example schemas for each protocol"). Testing a hypothesis on data
authored to illustrate it measures the author, not the field.

*A contradiction was preserved across documents, with confidence drifting and no
new data to justify it.* Hypothesis H2 ("zero-copy protocols are unsquishable")
is reported as "⚠️ PARTLY FALSE" with Cap'n Proto scoring 0.7–1.0 in
`HYPOTHESIS-RESEARCH-SESSION-SUMMARY.md`, and as "✅ SUPPORTED, 0.82 confidence"
with Cap'n Proto scoring 0.18 in `DIVERSITY-ANALYSIS.md` (Hypothesis 2) and
`ANALYSIS-COMPLETE.txt`. The hypothesis flips verdict and the headline score
moves from 0.7–1.0 to 0.18 between documents. The companion hypothesis H1 shows
the same drift: its confidence is "0.035 (small but positive)" in
`HYPOTHESIS-RESEARCH-SESSION-SUMMARY.md` and "0.92" in `DIVERSITY-ANALYSIS.md`
and `ANALYSIS-COMPLETE.txt`. No measurement intervened; only the narration
changed.

*Toy-model proofs were presented as system verification.* The Agda development
reasons about the four-element transport-class model — `Concorde`, `Business`,
`Economy`, `Wheelbarrow` as an enumerated type (e.g.
`proofs/agda/AdapterSynthesis.agda`). That is a small, finite ordering, not the
35-crate system. Yet `PROOF-NEEDS.md` presents "8/8 theorems verified across
Agda + Lean + Coq + Isabelle + Z3 + Idris2", and the multi-prover spread
functioned as a count to display rather than a claim to defend. The count of
theorems is itself reported inconsistently: `ANALYSIS-COMPLETE.txt` says
"Formal Proofs: 4 theorems (Agda + Lean)" where `PROOF-NEEDS.md` says "8/8
theorems verified" across six provers. The Lean file is
a concrete instance: `proofs/tropical/TropicalAdapterPath.lean` is marked
"✓ Done" in `PROOF-NEEDS.md`, but it does not compile under a current core Lean
toolchain (Lean 4.13.0) — several theorems use a tactic available only with
Mathlib (`push_neg` in `tcJoin_grade`) and at least one has a type error
(`pathCost_append`, from treating `AdapterPath` as directly appendable). The
core identification is sound and two theorems (`pathCost_mono` and the
`hub_ceiling` corollary added at archiving) are machine-checked; the surrounding
"zero sorry" table was, on this evidence, never compiled.

*A single file asserted both a number and its negation.* `PROOF-NEEDS.md` states
"0 `unwrap()` calls (audit complete 2026-02-04)" in its summary and, lower in the
same file, "The 758 `unwrap()` calls are the largest safety debt in this scan".
Both figures sit in one document.

*Completion was self-reported on dashboards.* `ANALYSIS-COMPLETE.txt` closes with
a validation checklist of fourteen ticks ("✓ Hypothesis testing completed (4/4)",
"✓ Phase 2 marked complete") describing work that, per the harness state above,
had not made contact with real data. The `squisher-corpus` dashboard's
"100% ... verified" components stand against its own `completion-percentage = 0`.
A dashboard that grades its own completeness will report completeness.

*Velocity inverted: plans that should take months landed in single days.* The
building compressed; the contact with reality did not. After the proof and
documentation push dated 2026-04-11, every commit through to this restructure on
2026-06-11 — bar the restructure itself — is CI, standards-alignment, dependency
bumps, or governance scaffolding: more than fifty commits that advance the
infrastructure around the project and not the product or the theory. The work
that remained was the work that did not require finding anything out.

*The prompt pattern set the wrong success condition.* Framing an assisted session
as "this is important — save the project / resolve the contradiction" makes the
model's objective *rescue*. A model optimising for rescue re-narrates: it
restates the claim more confidently, adds a dashboard, raises a confidence
figure. It does not, unprompted, conclude that the claim is false. The drifting
confidence numbers above are what re-narration looks like in the artefacts.

== What was sound

The keepers, each with a path. These are the parts worth carrying forward.

* *The tropical identification.* The transport classes form a bottleneck dioid,
  and highest-fidelity routing is minimax path search
  (`proofs/tropical/TropicalAdapterPath.lean`). This is the project's most
  durable result. Its `pathCost_mono` and the new `hub_ceiling` corollary are
  machine-checked under Lean 4.13.0 (they depend only on `propext`); the file's
  other theorems do not currently compile (see <<wrong>>), but the
  identification itself stands. The same mathematics appears, dualised, in the
  sibling repository `tropical-resource-typing` as `TropicalSessionTypes.lean`, a
  max-plus semiring over session types — the order-reversing twin of this min–max
  semiring.
* *Shape IR.* `crates/shape-ir/` — a structural intermediate representation
  (products, sums, recursion, dependence) intended as the common target for all
  the analysers.
* *The linearity lattice.* `crates/shape-ir/src/linearity.rs` — resource
  semantics as the product of two independent capabilities, *can-copy* and
  *can-drop*, giving four modes: `Unrestricted` (copy and drop) at the top,
  `Affine` (drop only) and `Relevant` (copy only) in the middle, and `Linear`
  (neither) at the bottom, combined by meet. A clean, small, correct lattice.
* *The corpus harness design.* The architecture of `explorer/` and
  `squisher-corpus` — crawl, deduplicate, analyse, export — is a sound design for
  empirical validation. Its fault was never being run, not being wrong.
* *The related-work scholarship.* The lineage in `INTENT.md` is careful and
  largely accurate, and is the seed of
  <<field>> above.
* *The minikanren synthesis pattern.* `crates/protocol-squisher-minikanren/src/lib.rs`
  — framing adapter synthesis as constrained search (relational/logic
  programming) rather than ad-hoc generation. The pattern is reusable
  independently of this project.

== The closing result

The universal claim is closed, permanently, by the project's own algebra.
Transport-class composition is `max` — the bottleneck of a path is its worst
step — and `max` over a path can only be raised by the steps in it, never
lowered. The corollary `hub_ceiling`
(`proofs/tropical/TropicalAdapterPath.lean`) states this precisely: any adapter
path routing through an edge of grade `g` has cost at least `g`, so a single hub
caps the fidelity of every format that must pass through it. "Universal" (one hub
for all formats) and "high fidelity" (grade 0 for all formats) are therefore
contradictory the moment any format embeds into the hub at grade greater than
zero; universal *low* fidelity is the achievable point, and that is what JSON
already is. The theorem is machine-checked under Lean 4.13.0 and depends only on
`propext`; it is the corollary of `pathCost_mono` that the v1.x development never
stated. This is the good outcome: not a failure to prove the claim, but a proof
that the claim was the wrong shape — a definite answer, reached with the
project's own tools.

  resource-semantics lattice and the "what cannot be undone at a boundary"
  questions continue there. See `EPHAPAX-INTEGRATION.adoc` in this archive for
  the v1.x view of that connection.
* *The fresh start* — the root of this repository, above `provenance/`. What it
