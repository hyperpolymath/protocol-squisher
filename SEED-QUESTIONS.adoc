= Protocol Squisher — Seed Questions

Questions whose answers would fix the scope of any successor. They are open
questions, not proposals. The history that prompts them is in
`provenance/PROVENANCE.adoc`.

. Now that the hub-ceiling result has closed the _universal_ framing, is there a
  successor that still wants universality, or does every viable one target a
  single, named pair of formats?

. Should a successor be built on each format's official upstream parser
  (grammar-faithful), given that the v1.x analysers were regex- and line-based?

. Is the durable artefact a tool at all, or is it the vocabulary — the transport
  grades, or the can-copy × can-drop linearity lattice
  (`provenance/crates/shape-ir/src/linearity.rs`)? If it is the vocabulary,
  should it be contributed to a boundary that already exists (buf, CUE, Arrow,
  a lens or optics library) rather than re-implemented here?

. Is there standalone demand for a schema-bloat linter — the one empirically
  suggestive thread, that evolution formats accrete optional and deprecated
  fields — independent of any conversion feature?

. What is the smallest claim a successor could make that would _not_ require a
  corpus to support — something validated by construction, or by a handful of
  real schemas?

. If empirical validation is required, who runs the corpus harness
  (`provenance/explorer/`, and the sibling `squisher-corpus`) to completion, on
  what real schemas, and what result would falsify the core claim?

. Should the tropical / bottleneck identification continue in this repository,
  or move wholesale to `tropical-resource-typing`, where its max-plus twin
  already lives?

. Does the linearity lattice belong to this project, or to `ephapax`, where
  resource semantics are the main subject?

. What would make automated adapter synthesis (the miniKanren approach,
  `provenance/crates/protocol-squisher-minikanren/src/lib.rs`) worth more than
  hand-writing the few adapters anyone actually needs?

. Who is the user, concretely, and what conversion do they perform by hand today
  that a successor would replace?

. What single measurement would show that a successor works, and can it be taken
  before any code is written?

. Is "nothing at all" — treating the question as answered and closing the
  repository — an acceptable outcome? If not, what specifically remains
  unfinished?
